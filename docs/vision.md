# Product Vision

## The Problem

People accumulate decades of experiences, relationships, turning points, and personal growth — but have no structured way to see it all, understand it, or share it meaningfully. Existing tools are fragmented:

- **Journals** capture moments but don't connect them
- **Social media** performs identity rather than exploring it
- **Memoir tools** are focused on output (a book) rather than ongoing understanding
- **AI journals** offer reflection prompts but lack structure and narrative

Nobody has built the thing that sits underneath all of these: **a structured life model**.

## The Opportunity

> Turn a person's life into a living, explorable identity map.

Not "record your life." Not "journal more." Instead:

**Understand your past. See your present clearly. Build your future more intentionally.**

## Core Insight

Most apps store moments. Threadline explains a life.

The value isn't in the data — it's in the **connections**:

- One childhood event shaped 20 later decisions
- A recurring fear of rejection drove career choices for a decade
- A friendship that started at 14 is still the emotional anchor at 35
- Three "rebuilding" eras share the same triggers and the same coping patterns

When the app understands events, relationships, traits, beliefs, places, goals, themes, and turning points — and how they connect — it can generate insights no other tool can.

## Positioning

**Not** "social journaling"

**Instead:**

- A living autobiography with intelligence
- A private life OS
- A digital self-portrait across time

## Emotional Promise

> "Understand your past, see your present clearly, and build your future more intentionally."

## Target Users (V1)

- Reflective adults (25–45) who think about personal growth
- People going through transitions (career change, parenthood, divorce, relocation)
- Anyone who has thought "I should write my story down someday"
- People who journal but want more structure and meaning from it

## What Success Looks Like

Users feel **emotionally attached** to their Threadline. It becomes the single place that holds the full picture of who they are. They return not because of notifications or streaks, but because it genuinely helps them understand themselves.

## Competitive Landscape

| Category | Examples | What they do well | What they miss |
|---|---|---|---|
| Timeline journals | Day One, Grid Diary | Daily logging, photo capture | No narrative structure, no identity modeling |
| Memoir tools | StoryWorth, Artifact | Guided prompts for stories | Output-focused (book), not ongoing reflection |
| AI journals | Rosebud, Reflectr | Smart prompts, AI summaries | No life model, no long-term pattern tracking |
| Private social | Cocoon, Locket | Sharing with close people | No self-understanding layer |

**Threadline's moat:** The structured life model underneath — once built, it powers summaries, pattern detection, personality evolution, memoir exports, and family archives. Nobody else has this layer.

## Social Philosophy

Start private. Add sharing gradually.

1. **Private autobiography tool** — the core experience must be excellent alone
2. **Family/couple sharing** — share selected parts with trusted people
3. **Collaborative memory** — let others contribute their perspective on shared events
4. **Curated public profiles** — a living autobiography page, not a feed
5. **Small reflection communities** — invite-only Circles for sharing turning points and lessons

The rule: **no performing identities, only exploring them.**

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Too much work to reconstruct a life | Guided prompts, AI interviews, autofill from dates/photos, "add memory" quick flow |
| Feels clinical or therapy-ish | Warm visual design, optional depth, insightful tone not preachy |
| Becomes fake-social (polished trauma summaries) | Private-first, no public feeds, sharing is selective and intentional |
| Users don't return after initial setup | Letters across time (future unlock), AI-generated insights that evolve, life dashboard that changes |

## Long-Term Vision

Threadline becomes the thing people pass down:

- Export to a book or documentary-style site
- Family archive spanning generations
- Memory vault for children
- Living family tree with real stories

The product starts as a personal tool and becomes a **legacy artifact**.
